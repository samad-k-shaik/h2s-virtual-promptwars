# Voter Registration Process in India

To vote in India, your name must be included in the Electoral Roll (Voters' List). Here is the process to register:

## Methods of Registration

1.  **Online Application (Voter Portal/NVSP):**
    *   Visit the official Voter Service Portal (voters.eci.gov.in) or download the Voter Helpline App (VHA).
    *   Register or log in using your mobile number.
    *   Fill out **Form 6** (Application for New Voters).
    *   Upload required documents.

2.  **Offline Application:**
    *   Download Form 6 from the ECI website or obtain it from the Electoral Registration Officer (ERO) or Booth Level Officer (BLO) in your area.
    *   Fill out the form and attach self-attested copies of the required documents.
    *   Submit the form to your local ERO, Assistant ERO, or BLO.

## Required Documents

When applying (online or offline), you typically need to provide proof of:

*   **Identity and Age**: Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Driving License, Passport, or Birth Certificate.
*   **Address (Ordinary Residence)**: Aadhaar Card, Passport, recent utility bills (electricity, water, gas), bank passbook, or rent agreement.
*   **Photograph**: A recent passport-size color photograph.

## Verification Process

1.  After submission, a Booth Level Officer (BLO) may visit the provided address for physical verification.
2.  You can track your application status online using the reference ID generated during submission.

## Voter ID Card (EPIC)

Once your application is approved and your name is added to the electoral roll, an Electors Photo Identity Card (EPIC) will be generated and typically sent via post to your registered address. You can also download an electronic version (e-EPIC) from the Voter Portal.
