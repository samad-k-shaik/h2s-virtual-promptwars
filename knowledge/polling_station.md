# Polling Station Procedures

Voting at the polling station involves a systematic process to ensure security and secrecy. Here is what to expect on polling day:

## Preparation Before Polling Day

1.  **Find Your Polling Booth**: Ensure your name is on the voter list and know your designated polling booth. You can find this online via the Voter Portal or Voter Helpline App using your EPIC number.
2.  **Bring Voter Slip & ID**: Carry your official Voter Information Slip (if received) and a valid photo ID (like your Voter ID Card (EPIC), Aadhaar, Passport, Driving License, etc.). Note that the Voter Information Slip alone is *not* sufficient as an identity proof.

## Steps at the Polling Station

1.  **First Polling Officer**: 
    *   This officer checks your name on the electoral roll and verifies your identity document.
    *   They will call out your name and serial number so that polling agents of political parties can note it down.

2.  **Second Polling Officer**:
    *   They mark your left forefinger with indelible ink.
    *   They hand you a voter slip and ask you to sign (or put a thumb impression) on Form 17A (the register of voters).

3.  **Third Polling Officer**:
    *   They collect the voter slip given by the second officer.
    *   They press the "Ballot" button on the Control Unit of the EVM to activate the voting machine in the voting compartment.

4.  **Voting Compartment (Secrecy)**:
    *   Proceed to the voting compartment where the Ballot Unit (BU) and VVPAT machine are kept.
    *   Press the blue button on the Ballot Unit next to the candidate and symbol of your choice.
    *   **Verify**: After pressing the button, look at the VVPAT (Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail) machine window. A paper slip showing the candidate's serial number, name, and symbol will be visible behind the glass for about 7 seconds before it drops into the sealed drop box.
    *   A beep sound confirms your vote has been successfully recorded.

## Important Rules

*   Maintain the secrecy of your vote. Do not take photos or videos inside the polling station.
*   Mobile phones and electronic devices are generally not allowed inside the polling booth.
