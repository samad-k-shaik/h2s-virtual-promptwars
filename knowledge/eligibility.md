# Voter Eligibility in India

To be eligible to vote in Indian elections (Lok Sabha and State Legislative Assemblies), a citizen must meet the following criteria:

## Core Requirements

1.  **Citizenship**: Must be a citizen of India. Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) who hold Indian citizenship are also eligible to vote but must register in the constituency where their passport address is located.
2.  **Age**: Must be 18 years of age or older on the qualifying date (usually January 1st, April 1st, July 1st, or October 1st of the year of the electoral roll revision).
3.  **Residency**: Must be an ordinary resident of the polling area (constituency) where they wish to enroll.

## Disqualifications

A person is disqualified from voting if they are:

*   Not a citizen of India.
*   Declared to be of unsound mind by a competent court.
*   Disqualified due to corrupt practices or other offenses relating to elections under the law.

## Important Note

Merely meeting the eligibility criteria does not automatically allow you to vote. **You must be enrolled in the Electoral Roll (Voters' List)** of your constituency to cast your ballot.
